History

he Dinagyang Festival is a vibrant cultural and religious celebration in Iloilo City, Philippines, honoring the Santo Niño and commemorating the historic arrival of Malay settlers and their pact with the indigenous Ati people. The term "Dinagyang" is derived from the Hiligaynon word "dágyang," which means "merrymaking." This reflects the festival's joyful and spirited nature, as it showcases the rich cultural heritage and community spirit of the Ilonggo people. The festival is celebrated annually on the fourth Sunday of January and is known for its colorful parades, lively street dancing, and elaborate costumes that transform Iloilo City into a hub of festivity. In 1977, the festival was officially renamed Dinagyang, distinguishing it from other festivals like the Kalibo Ati-Atihan and Sinulog Festival. The festival commemorates the Barter of Panay, a legendary event where the Malay settlers negotiated with the Ati people for land, symbolizing the peaceful coexistence and cultural exchange between the two groups.
